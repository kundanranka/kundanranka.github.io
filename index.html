Journey

1930-1980

Our journey as traditional medicine practitioners began with our ancestors in the 1930. First in the list is Subbhamma patti. She practiced as gramma vaidyar (village traditional medicine practitioner) for more than 50 years (1930-1980). She treated patients for liver diseases (such as jaundice), skin diseases (psoriasis, rashes), poisonous bites (such as centipede, rat and snake bites). She first began her practice in Poovanur village later during her late 50’s she shifted to Mettupalayam, Tiruppur district along with her husband Venkatachalam chettiar, who was also a gramma vaidyar and known for his free of cost treatments during the period 1930-1978.

1945-1992

The traditional treatment meathords was passed hereditarily to their children Kandasamy chettiar and Muthammal. Kandasamy chettiar was gramma vaidyar in Mettupalayam, Tiruppur district from 1964 -1990. He treated patient’s for liver diseases, skin diseases, poisonous bites and arthritis while Muthammal married and settled in Kambiliyampatti village near Kangayam town. She consulted and practiced in the village from 1945-1992. She also followed the same treatment method as her parents and was a specialist in treating patients for skin diseases (psoriasis, boils, chicken pox, rashes), labor and liver diseases. This practice was then taken over by her son Subbukutty for 27 years (1975-2012) in Kambiliyampatti village. Along with him practised K.Rangasamy who is son of Kandasamy chettiar and grandson of Subbhamma patti.

2003

K. Rangasamy started practice as hereditary traditional medicine practitioner in the year 2003 in Kambiliyampatti village and later settled in Palani. He is a specialist in treating patients for fertility, obesity, hyperglycemia, psychiatric disorders, liver diseases (jaundice stage I,II & III), skin diseases (psoriasis, rashes), lung diseases.
